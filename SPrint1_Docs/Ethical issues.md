Introduction
Software development is associated with various ethical issues. This document presents issues identified regarding Book/Report swap application and possible ways to mitigate them.
User Privacy and Personal Data
Due to the nature of application, various personal data might be collected, such as names, contact information, address, etc. That raises an ethical concern regarding the amount of data being stored as well as their security [1]. Studies suggest that in user-generated communities, fear of personal information exposure shapes user’s behaviour on the platform [2]. 
As an attempt to mitigate this, during the design of our application, we will implement systems that collect as little information as possible (for example, city only, rather than the full address). Moreover, pseudonymised data only will be displayed publicly (user name, rather than the full name). Additionally, the way data is used will be explained clearly, with users being able to control their privacy settings.
Safety and Trust in User Interactions
As swapping implies a direct exchange between users, it creates concerns of physical safety or unsafe meeting arrangements. Research shows that to mitigate this, digital platforms could integrate “trust and safety” functions [3].
To ensure that platform we are developing is as safe as possible, we will offer guidelines for safe swaps (like meeting in public space, or sending items to post lockers), keep a messaging inside the app, but with blocking/reporting mechanisms.
Content Moderation and Online Harm
Other issues are the safety issues connected to poor or lack of content moderation, that can lead to users posting inappropriate or offensive content, or other type of harmful material.
Research show that companies of various sizes struggle to find balance between freedom of expression and community safety [4]. 
As an attempt to provide safe environment for users, we will define community guidelines and offer reporting tools. We will also include basic automated moderation.
Accessibility and Inclusivity
Additional issue is that users might have various abilities.
Ethical design research identifies accessibility as a major concern in app development and encourages designs accommodating diverse users [5]. 
As an attempt to provide as inclusive an accessible environment, we’ll ensure to use accessible UI, such as (but not limited to): high contrast text, alt-text for images). We’ll also test our website against accessibility standards.
Intellectual Property and Copyright
User-generated content within the platform contributes to the construction of a digital persona for each user [6]
Research on persona rights and user-generated content highlights the importance of allowing users meaningful control over representations of their identity and contributions.[6]
To ensure that, we’re provide a user with control over their content, such as ability to edit or delete listings, images, or deactivate and remove their account. We’ll also explain what happens with user’s content after upload and deletion.
Summary
Above are all the ethical issues identified by us. However, we are allowing the possibility of adding more issues to consider as we move to design and development phases.
